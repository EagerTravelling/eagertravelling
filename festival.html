Festival

India

Holi Festival

Holi, also known as the Celebration of Spring, the Festival of Colors, or the Festival of Love, is a renowned ancient Hindu festival. The event honours Radha Krishna's eternal and holy love. Holi is a harvest celebration that heralds the start of spring and the end of the winter season.
Location: India.
Festival Dates: 17 March — 18 March 2022







Thailand

Songkran Festival

Songkran has been the traditional Thai New Year holiday. It is a festival that promotes friendliness, love, compassion, and gratitude via the use of water as a medium of expression. The name Songkran comes from Sanskrit and means "to move onward." Water tossing, ritual washing of Buddha figures, processions, and performances are all part of the event. Throughout the festivities, a delectable selection of traditional meals is served.
Location: Chiang Mai, Phuket and Bangkok
Festival Dates: 13 April — 15 April 2022



China

The Dragon Boat Festival

Duan Yang, which meaning "Upright Sun" or "Double Fifth," is another name for the Dragon Boat Festival (Duan Wu Jie). The Dragon Boat Festival is held every year to honour Qu Yuan's rescue effort. Locals started pouring sacrificial cooked rice into the river for Qu Yuan, believing that the rice would keep the river fish from consuming Qu Yuan's body. Zongzi is the Dragon Boat Festival's traditional meal.
Location: China
Festival Dates: 3rd June 2022





U.S

Kutztown Folk Festival

The Kutztown Folk Festival is the country's longest continually running folklife festival. This nine-day festival attracts tourists from all over the world, delighting families while also giving significant insight into the Pennsylvania Dutch customs and way of life. Authentic folklife demonstrations, a wide variety of Pennsylvania Dutch food, over 200 nationally recognised, juried folk artists and traditional American artisans, over 2,500 locally handmade Dutch quilts on display and for sale, antiques and collectibles, and six stages of entertainment are among the highlights of the Kutztown Festival.
Location: Kutztown, Pennsylvania
Festival Dates: 2nd July — 10th July, 2022
